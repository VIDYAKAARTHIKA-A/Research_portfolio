# 🔬 Research Exploration

## Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)

Conducted extensive study and comparative analysis of adversarial generative modeling frameworks with focus on:
- training stability,
- reconstruction fidelity,
- latent representation learning,
- and generative performance optimization.

Deeply studied 25+ GAN architectures including:
- DCGAN
- CycleGAN
- WGAN
- PatchGAN
- Pix2Pix
- U-Net based GANs
- Progressive GAN
- SRGAN
- ESRGAN
- DeblurGAN

Explored:
- discriminator-generator dynamics,
- adversarial optimization,
- perceptual losses,
- mode collapse,
- Wasserstein optimization,
- and convergence behavior.

Analyzed architectural optimization strategies including:
- changing convolutional layer configurations,
- increasing network depth,
- residual connections,
- skip connections,
- normalization techniques,
- and patch-based discriminators.

Studied the impact of these modifications on:
- image quality,
- training stability,
- PSNR,
- perceptual fidelity,
- and reconstruction performance.

## Comparative Analysis of GANs and Diffusion Models

Conducted comparative analysis between:
- adversarial generative models,
- and diffusion-based generative systems.

Explored differences in:
- training stability,
- sample diversity,
- inference speed,
- image fidelity,
- mode coverage,
- and scalability.

Studied why diffusion models increasingly outperform GANs in:
- diversity preservation,
- stable training,
- and high-fidelity image synthesis.

Analyzed tradeoffs between:
- fast adversarial generation,
- and iterative denoising-based generation frameworks.
